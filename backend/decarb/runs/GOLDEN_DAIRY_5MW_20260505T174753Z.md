## §1 Executive Summary  ![status](https://img.shields.io/badge/status-IMPLEMENTED%20v0-green)

Cumbria Dairy Co. (synthetic) is a food drink operation in Carlisle with an annual primary energy demand of **50.5 GWh**. The current baseline is dominated by natural-gas-fired steam raising (38.0 GWh/yr gas; 12.5 GWh/yr electricity).

**Baseline Scope 1+2 emissions (location-based):** 8,851 tCO₂e/yr.
**Including Scope 3 upstream gas (well-to-tank + electricity T&D losses):** 10,395 tCO₂e/yr.

The technology screen retained **2 site-applicable candidates from a longlist of 16** (9 excluded by site temperature/sector pre-filter; of the 7 that reached the 9-axis decision tree, 3 were excluded on process-compatibility, infrastructure, or client-constraint grounds, and **2** routed to a senior grid-decision register because their estimated electrical demand exceeds 1.5× the site's declared headroom; see §3).

Investment-pathway sequencing, Monte-Carlo NPV bands, pinch analysis, formal grid-connection assessment and reliability sizing are deferred to subsequent engine releases (see roadmap badges below).

### §3.1 Shortlist (2 retained)

**1. Industrial Heat Pump — hot water at 85°C** — `industrial_heat_pump_mid_temp` (heat pump)
Capacity range: 200–1,930 kW.
> Thermodynamically feasible: HP delivers 85°C with NH3/R1234ze(E). HP covers 1287 kW avg demand; commercial range 200–15,000 kW/train. Mid-temp HP (85°C) achievable with single-stage screw using NH3 or R1234ze(E). Temperature lift from ambient/waste-heat source typically 50–70 K — within single-stage screw envelope (PR < 7). Reference: BS EN 14825:2022. Technology is compatible with UK ETS position and CCA targets. Electrification reduces Scope 1 gas emissions, improving CCA performance. No new ETS obligation introduced.

*Flagged risks:*
- R717 (Ammonia) is the likely refrigerant for this temperature tier: toxic (TLV-TWA 25 ppm), requires BS EN 378-3 plant-room ventilation and NH3 leak detection; confirm charge-limit per BS EN 378-1 Annex C for occupied-zone proximity
- Natural refrigerants (NH3, R744, R290) are exempt from F-Gas Regulation 517/2014 quota and phase-down requirements — preferred for new industrial plant

**2. Thermal Energy Storage (sensible-heat tank)** — `thermal_energy_storage` (thermal storage)
Capacity range: 100–100,000 kW.
> TES is a storage buffer, thermodynamically compatible with any temperature tier. TES capacity scalable to match demand; vessel sizing by detailed design. Compressor envelope check: not applicable for this technology type. Technology is compatible with UK ETS position and CCA targets. Electrification reduces Scope 1 gas emissions, improving CCA performance. No new ETS obligation introduced.

### §5.2 Regulatory exposure

**UK ETS.** This site is NOT in UK ETS scope (combustion below the 20 MW threshold and not in the regulated activities list). No ETS allowance liability. Monitor scope expansion proposals (DESNZ consultations 2024–25) — stationary combustion above 10 MW has been floated for inclusion.
**SECR.** This site is SECR-reportable (size thresholds met). Annual disclosure required of Scope 1, Scope 2 location-based, an intensity metric (typically tCO2e per unit of production or per £m turnover), and the energy-efficiency actions taken in the year. Reference: Companies (Directors' Report) and Limited Liability Partnerships (Energy and Carbon Report) Regulations 2018.
**CCA.** This site participates in a Climate Change Agreement under sub-sector 'dairy_processing'. The CCL reduced rate applies (see CCL block: ~92% relief on electricity, ~89% on gas, per HMRC 2024+). CCA targets typically tighten at 2-year intervals; achievement is measured against an energy-intensity baseline (kWh per tonne / hectolitre / unit). Maintain target compliance to retain the relief. Reference: HMRC CCL guidance and sector trade-body CCA.
**CBAM.** This site is NOT CBAM-exposed (no listed goods exported to the EU). No CBAM declaration or charge applies. Monitor UK CBAM (live from 2027) for any sub-sector additions.
**CCL.** Estimated annual CCL liability **£23,968**. CCA reduced rates (HMRC 2024+): elec 0.062 p/kWh × 12.5M kWh = £7,750; gas 0.043 p/kWh × 38.0M kWh = £16,218.
Without the CCA, the gross main-rate liability would be **£244,315/yr**, so participation in the CCA saves approximately **£220,347/yr** — a material commercial reason to maintain CCA targets.
